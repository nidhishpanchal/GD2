Rick Ross has a reputation for mentioning foods in his rap lyrics and making songs after food. Sometimes they have meaning, sometimes it is just for fun. What is evident though, is his love for food. Rick Ross’ relationship with Lemon Pepper Wings first started when he was the promotional figure for Wingstop when they released this flavour. Flash forward to today, he now owns 25 Wingstop locations and was featured on Drake's Lemon Pepper Freestyle’ on Scary Hours 2. Here is a healthier air fried alternative recipe for this iconic flavour.


Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs. chicken wings, trimmed, separated, and wing tips discarded
  • 1 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 2 tsp. lemon pepper, divided
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt, divided
  • 2 Tbsp. unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 Tbsp. honey mustard, for dipping (optional)

Steps:

    1. Preheat basket of an air fryer to 425°F for 15 minutes. Pat chicken wings dry.
    2. Toss together wings, olive oil, 1 teaspoon of the lemon pepper, and 1/2 of the teaspoon salt in a medium bowl.
    3. Place wings in a single layer in preheated air fryer basket and cook 8 minutes.
    4. Turn wings over, and cook until golden brown and mostly cooked, about 8 minutes.
    5. Remove wings from air fryer to a clean bowl; toss with melted butter and remaining 1 teaspoon lemon pepper and 1/2 teaspoon salt.
    6. Return wings to air fryer basket, and cook at 400°F until deep golden brown and crispy, 3 to 5 minutes.
    7. Serve with honey mustard dipping sauce, if desired.
